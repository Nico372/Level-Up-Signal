Signal of the month

LinkedIn & Healthcare 2026: Key Trends for Healthcare Marketers

154 million LinkedIn members engage with healthcare content — and HCPs are 28% more likely to research healthcare products on LinkedIn than on any other social platform. Thought Leader Ads deliver 1.7x higher CTR than standard formats, and CTV via LinkedIn is emerging as a new way to reach clinicians outside the office.

Why it matters: Direct, actionable data for GE HealthCare teams targeting healthcare professionals and hospital decision-makers on LinkedIn — organic reach is no longer enough.

The broader trend: LinkedIn's dominance in healthcare professional engagement reflects a wider shift across this month's top stories: the era of passive, broadcast-style digital marketing is over. Whether it's pharma marketers reallocating search budgets toward specialised platforms, Meta overtaking Google on AI-powered automation, or programmatic systems evolving into agentic decision-makers — the common thread is precision over reach. For MedTech brands like GE HealthCare, this means leaning into platforms and formats that deliver the right message to the right professional at the right moment, backed by first-party data and measurable outcomes.

Question of the month

As AI agents increasingly handle programmatic buying and campaign optimisation, what should marketing teams at a company like GE HealthCare actually be doing differently — and what human judgment remains irreplaceable?

Our take: The shift to agentic media relocates strategic work, it doesn't eliminate it. AI optimises toward the goals you set, with the data you feed it, within guardrails you define. For MedTech teams, the highest-value work is now upstream: sharper audience definitions, cleaner first-party data, and business outcomes as KPIs — not campaign metrics. Healthcare-specific constraints (regulation, HCP targeting complexity, long multi-stakeholder sales cycles) are where human judgment stays irreplaceable.
